How-to

Row

Interact with the graphs!

Data are updated daily whenever they become available (date printed on the navigation bar shows when the data were last updated). All graphs (except the few trend lines on the Daily summary homepage ) on this site are interactive. Here are a few tips to help you make the most of these graphs.

First , view the graphs on your computer screen whenever possible. You will be able to hover your cursor over each graph to see the y-value (e.g., number of vaccinations each day) corresponding to a specific date. You will still be able to do this if you are using a smartphone, except that its no longer mouseover that youre using. Instead, you do this by tapping (sliding across the time series does not work) the part of curve/ line of the graph where you want the corresponding y-value to be shown (I for one feel this is no easy feat). if the value is not displayed, and youre sure that youre tapping within the plot margins, applying just a little more pressure on your touchscreen might do the trick.

Second, youll notice a horizontal slider just beneath the x-axis of each graph. Use it to specify the date range if you want to zoom in on a particular period of the pandemic. Note that the graphs may sometimes disappear, especially if the system detects inactivity. A simple refresh should resolve the issue. More functions and plots may be added in the near future so keep checking back.

If youre interested in my work and wish to connect with me, or if you have any queries or suggestions, you can contact me through several means (click here or About me to go to my GitHub page; contact details are listed there). Happy exploring!

Positivity Rate |
What share of tests comes back positive each day?

Latest data provided on 16 May 2021

6.6%
Daily positivity rate.

5.54%
7-day moving average of positivity rate.


Positivity rate is equal to the number of positive tests, i.e., tests that come back positive, divided by the total number of tests conducted (PCR & antigen rapid tests) within the past 24 hours. As a general rule of thumb, positivity rate of less than 5% means theres an adequate level of testing relative to the size of the outbreak, or the epidemic is under control in a country.

Case Fatality Ratio |
What is the ratio between confirmed deaths and confirmed cases?

Latest data provided on 16 May 2021

0.4%
Case Fatality Ratio (CFR) or more commonly known as Case Fatality Rate.

CFR is the total number of deaths divided by the total number of confirmed cases. Despite being a commonly used measure of mortality from a disease, it has a number of important limitations and underlying assumptions which call for careful interpretation, especially during a fast-moving pandemic.
